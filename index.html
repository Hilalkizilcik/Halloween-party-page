About

The origin of Halloween is the Samhain Festival, celebrated by the pagan Celts in ancient Britain. The Celts considered November 1 the end of summer and the beginning of winter. The origin of the word samhain is based on the Old Irish words sam (summer) and fuinfuin (end). At this date, the herds were returning from the highlands, and the land lease agreements were being renewed with the landlords. On this day it was also believed that the spirits of the dead visit the houses where they lived in the past. Great fires were lit on the hills to ignite the hearths in the houses and at the same time keep evil spirits away. People wore masks and costumes to avoid being recognized by the spirits they believed were walking around. Because of these traditions, Samhain Samhain festival has become synonymous with witches, goblins, fairies and demons over time.
